Vidigal is my home: the favela over the sea, squeezed between Leblon and the
Dois Irmãos rock. And it is where I take anyone who wants to understand why I
chose Rio — especially in the late afternoon, when the light drops and the
city switches on.

## How to get there

We start from Praça do Vidigal, the entrance to the community. From there we
warm up the engines right away: the local mototaxi riders take us to the
upper part — revitalised, with the football pitch and the park. On weekdays
the afternoon tour leaves at 15:30 — just the right timing to be up top with
the good light.

![Colourful "Vidigal 314" mural with the kites](../../../assets/photos/marquee-2.jpg)

## Where to look

In the upper part we stop at a bar with one of the most beautiful views in
the city: Leblon, Ipanema and Sugarloaf in a single frame. Then we walk down,
alley by alley, stopping where life happens. The grand finale is a panoramic
terrace facing Christ the Redeemer.
